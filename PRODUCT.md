# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, fellow developers, and the open source community — people who want to understand who Patrick is, what he builds, and how he thinks. They arrive from GitHub, LinkedIn, or a link shared in a PR. They're looking for signal: real projects, real code, real thinking.

## Product Purpose

A personal website that presents a professional identity through the lens of a sailing journey — precise, explorative, grounded. Every section is a waypoint: the hero sets the scene (open water, sky shifting with time of day), the about section charts the course so far, projects are the vessels built along the way, and contact is the invitation to sail together. Success is a visitor leaving with a clear sense of who Patrick is and what he ships.

## Brand Personality

Precise, explorative, grounded. Confident but not loud. Technical without jargon for its own sake. The voice carries the slogan "Turning ideas into innovative solutions" — not as a banner, but as a through-line. The site should feel like talking to a developer who cares about craft and isn't trying to impress you with flash.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS template aesthetics (Bootstrap/Tailwind corporate landing pages)
- Dark terminal bro (green-on-black hacker stereotype)
- Overdesigned agency sites (motion for motion's sake, complex illustrations)
- The sky-blue accent is a personal preference derived from a love of the sea, not a football club reference — the brand abstracts toward navigation and journey, not team colors

## Design Principles

1. **The journey shows, not tells** — every section is a waypoint. The hero is open water, the about section charts the course, projects are the vessels built. The sailing metaphor carries through without being literal.

2. **Precision over polish** — details earn their place. Contrast ratios, spacing rhythm, and semantic HTML come before decorative flourishes. Get the fundamentals right before adding any layer of ornament.

3. **Grounded in craft** — the work speaks for itself. Writing, open source contributions, and projects are the primary content. The design exists to frame them, not to compete with them.

4. **One sailor, one boat** — the scale is personal. Not a fleet, not a team. The site reflects an individual's journey, not a company or a movement. Intimate, direct, human.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA as the baseline (contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, semantic structure)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected throughout (no critical animation depends on motion)
- Light and dark themes, stored in sessionStorage
- Skip-to-content link for keyboard users
- No content gated behind JavaScript
